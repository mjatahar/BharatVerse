Kerala – God's Own Country

A narrow strip of evergreen wonders wedged between the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea, Kerala has been radiating culture, spice and serenity for over 3000 years.

Introduction

Geography & Climate

Spread across 38 863 km², Kerala enjoys a tropical monsoon climate. 44 rivers, 34 back-water lakes and 580 km of coastline create a unique labyrinth of waterways.

Spice Garden of India

Black-pepper, cardamom, clove, nutmeg, cinnamon—Kerala produces 97 % of India's pepper and 75 % of cardamom.

100 % Literate State

Kerala became the first Indian state to achieve universal literacy (1991). The Kerala model is studied globally.

History

3000 BCE – Sangam Era

Chera dynasty rules the Malabar coast; trade links with Babylon, Egypt and Rome flourish.

52 CE – Arrival of St. Thomas

One of the twelve apostles of Jesus is believed to have landed at Muziris.

7th – 9th Century – Adi Shankara

Philosopher-saint born at Kalady; consolidates Advaita Vedanta.

1498 – Vasco da Gama

Portuguese explorer lands at Kappad near Kozhikode.

1956 – State of Kerala

On 1 Nov 1956, the modern state is formed.

Cuisine

Sadhya – Vegetarian Feast

Served on fresh banana leaf, the 24-item spread includes parippu, sambar, avial, thoran, kalan, inji-puli, pappadam, payasam and red rice.

Seafood Specials

Karimeen Pollichathu—fish marinated with Kerala spices, wrapped in banana leaf and pan-grilled.

Malabar Biriyani & Pathiri

Thalassery biriyani uses short-grain kaima rice, fried onions, cashew and ghee.

Payasam Varieties

Palada, Parippu, Ada Pradhaman, Gothambu and Chakka payasam slow-cooked in uruli till creamy.

Traditional Attire

Kasavu & Set-Saree

Off-white cotton with golden jari border worn during Onam and Vishu.

Bridal Wear

Rich Kasaragod silk sarees with wide gold kasavu, paired with temple jewellery.

Mundu – Unisex Garment

4.5 m cotton cloth dhoti-style, edged with 5 cm coloured band.

Folk & Classical Arts

Kathakali

"Story-Play" born in 17th-century temples. Green faces denote heroes, red-black are demons.

Theyyam

800-year-old ritual dance of North-Kerala. Performers personify deities, heroes, serpent-gods.

Mohiniyattam

"Dance of the enchantress" – graceful swaying, circular movements and emotive eye-glances.

Koodiyattam

2000-year-old Sanskrit theatre recognised by UNESCO.

Thiruvathira Kali

Women dance in circle around nilavilakku on Thiruvathira day.

Pulikali

"Tiger-dance" of Thrissur – 200+ men painted as yellow-black tigers.

Music Traditions

Sopana Sangeetam

Temple music sung on the steps leading to sanctum. Slow, meditative ragas.

Panchavadyam

Orchestra of five instruments—timila, maddalam, ilathalam, kombu and idakka.

Mappila Pattu

Folk songs of Malabar Muslims, sung in Arabic-Malayalam script.

Paintings & Murals

Temple Murals

900-year-old frescoes at Padmanabhapuram palace depict episodes from Ramayana.

Kerala Tanjore

Local adaptation of Tanjore style—raised gold leaf, glass beads and gem tones.

Kalamezhuthu

Floor art drawn with natural powders during Theyyam worship.

Contributions to India

Ayurveda & Well-being

Kottakkal Arya Vaidya Sala globalised Ayurveda. 850+ licensed hospitals.

Spice & Maritime Trade

70 % of India's pepper export originates from Kochi & Thoothukudi ports.

Literacy & Education

Kerala's 96.2 % literacy fuels India's largest pool of nurses & doctors.

Vallam Kali – Boat Race

Nehru Trophy boat race pioneered sports tourism in India.

Responsible Tourism

Kerala won UNWTO Ulysses Award (2014) for inclusive tourism.

Aranmula Kannadi

Metal-alloy mirror handmade by 35 families of Aranmula; GI tagged.
